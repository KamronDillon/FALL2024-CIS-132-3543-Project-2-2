There are four key points about factoring quadratics: sum-product pattern, grouping method, Perfect square trinomial pattern, and difference of squares pattern.

We have four basic rules when solving exponents: zero exponent rule, product of powers rule, quotient of powers rule, and power of power rule.

It can be tricky with logarithms, so we have three basic rules to follow: the product rule, our quotient rule, and finally the all-powerful power rule.

When dealing with trigonometric identities we have three: Pythagorean identity, reciprocal identity, and quotient identity.
